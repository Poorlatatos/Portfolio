Introduction

The Co$t was a project that I had worked whilst in school and during that period, I and a few other group members took part in a competition called the SPF Delta Competition which was sanctioned by the Singapore Police Force as a way for them to get in touch with the youth by introducing them to games which centered around the idea of "Educating the youth about if they should break the law".

The game was developed in a span of 2 months and the development process was quite hectic as we had to juggle between school work and the game development. We had to come up with a game concept that was both fun and educational, and we had to come up with a game design that was both simple and effective in conveying the message of the game. We also had to come up with a game design that was both simple and effective in conveying the message of the game.

The premise of the game is the player is a student who has stolen from a convenience store and is being initially chased by the store owner. The player must run through the store and avoid the store owner and other obstacles in order to escape the store and get away with the stolen goods. Even though this is the premise, the outlook of the game is to be more educating and to show the consequences of stealing, as the player will be chased by the store owner and police and eventually will get caught.

Conception

Finally, as we were in the late stages of development, we had to scale down our ending scene. We initially had the idea of making the ending scene to be more akin to something like a full fleshed horror game, where the store owner would transform into a personfication of the consequences of stealing and would chase the player in a more intense manner, but we had to scale down our ambitions as we had a very limited time frame to work with and we had to come up with something that would still make sense and would still be able to convey the message of the game.

Overall, the development process was quite hectic and we had to juggle between school work and the game development, but it was a fun and rewarding experience as we were able to create a game that was both fun and educational.
