Introduction

Behind Closed Doors, a project which I had made for IMH (Institute Of Mental Health). This project mainly took place as a request from IMH for a prototype they would like to see students try their hands at it. I had the pleasure of working on this project with a few other groupmates whom I personally knew, which made the team work better and much more efficiently. The premise of the story that IMH gave us was about a girl whom used to be a star student but then suffered a sudden streak of unfortunate problems, which caused her to become depressed. As the mother of this girl, your goal is to figure out what problems might be afflicting your daughter. Given this premise, we worked on a mystery/ detective/ puzzle solving game. This allowed us to work with a whole bunch of puzzle making ideas like figuring out where to put down false bottom drawers and how to unlock them, by using a key or pressing a button, etc. Despite all of this, we had a huge problem which was figuring out how to navigate game development with VR Headsets. We were relatively new to VR development and struggled with the physics that came with it, collisions were a massive bug that caused so many issues especially when interacting two different object with one another like the key entering the keyhole. After a long and grueling 6 weeks of work, we managed to pull it off and finally produce a full fledge working VR game that impressed the IMH clients. This allowed us to work further on this project as of now for our final Capstone project.

Conception

We initially had the idea of making a game that was more akin to something like a full fleshed horror game, but we had to scale down our ambitions as we had a very limited time frame to work with and we had to come up with a game concept that was both fun and educational.

Conclusion

Finally, as we were in the late stages of development, we had to scale down our ending scene. We initially had the idea of making the ending scene to be more akin to something like a full fleshed horror game, where the store owner would transform into a personfication of the consequences of stealing and would chase the player in a more intense manner, but we had to scale down our ambitions as we had a very limited time frame to work with and we had to come up with something that would still make sense and would still be able to convey the message of the game.

Overall, the development process was quite hectic and we had to juggle between school work and the game development, but it was a fun and rewarding experience as we were able to create a game that was both fun and educational.
