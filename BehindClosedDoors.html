Introduction

Behind Closed Doors, a project which I had made for IMH (Institute Of Mental Health). This project mainly took place as a request from IMH for a prototype they would like to see students try their hands at it. I had the pleasure of working on this project with a few other groupmates whom I personally knew, which made the team work better and much more efficiently. The premise of the story that IMH gave us was about a girl whom used to be a star student but then suffered a sudden streak of unfortunate problems, which caused her to become depressed. As the mother of this girl, your goal is to figure out what problems might be afflicting your daughter. Given this premise, we worked on a mystery/ detective/ puzzle solving game. This allowed us to work with a whole bunch of puzzle making ideas like figuring out where to put down false bottom drawers and how to unlock them, by using a key or pressing a button, etc.

Development

Despite all of this, we had a huge problem which was figuring out how to navigate game development with VR Headsets. We were relatively new to VR development and struggled with the physics that came with it, collisions were a massive bug that caused so many issues especially when interacting two different object with one another like the key entering the keyhole. After a long and grueling 6 weeks of work, we managed to pull it off and finally produce a full fledge working VR game that impressed the IMH clients. This allowed us to work further on this project as of now for our final Capstone project.

Future For The Project

Moving forward, we plan to expand on the foundation we've built with Behind Closed Doors. Our goal is to refine the gameplay mechanics, enhance the narrative elements, and work on improving the overall user experience.
